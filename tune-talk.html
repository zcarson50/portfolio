TuneTalk Overview

Music is deeply social, but most streaming platforms emphasize consumption over conversation. While people share playlists, there isn’t a widely adopted platform for reviewing and discovering music through social interaction — the way Letterboxd did for film. TuneTalk explores this gap by combining music streaming with a social layer of reviews, ratings, and discovery.

Research

  • User Research: Conducted contextual inquiries into how subjects listen to music, what apps they use, points of struggle and confusion, and new features/fixes that they would like to see.
  • Market/Exemplar Research: Studied Letterboxd (film reviews) and Goodreads (books). Key inspiration: simple rating systems + community-driven critique.

Design Goals

  • Create a social hub for music reviews where users can rate, write reviews, and discuss music.
  • Help people discover music through peer recommendations.
  • Keep interface lightweight and mobile-friendly for quick posting and browsing.

Prototyping and Testing

  • Low-fidelity wireframes in Figma to map user flows.
  • High-fidelity prototype with album artwork, clean typography, and interaction cues.
  • Unmoderated and moderated user-testing: allowing users to go through the app, gathered their feedback

Insights and Learnings

  • Users value both depth and speed: short ratings for quick logging, long reviews for discussion.
  • Social recommendations drive discovery more than algorithms alone.
  • Playback integration with streaming APIs considered for MVP but deferred for scope.

Reflections and Post-Mortem

  • What worked: Concept validation — participants saw clear value over existing apps.
  • What didn’t: Original plan was too ambitious — complex review and playback features.
  • Takeaway: Social critique can meaningfully drive discovery; even small communities matter.
