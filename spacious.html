Spacious Overview

A simple application to help users to find and sell parking spots in their area

Initial Design Goals

  • Simplify the process of finding short-term or long-term parking in busy locations.
  • Provide clear, upfront pricing and availability before committing.
  • Create a fast, frictionless flow that works well on web or mobile in the moment of need.

Ideation

  • Scenarios: Last-minute parking before a concert (short-term); commuter reserving a monthly space (long-term).
  • Figuring out how users would be updated on open spots, how to incentivize those updates

Prototyping and Testing

  • Developed a high-fidelity prototype in highlighting real-time availability and upfront pricing.
  • Conducted usability testing: focused on how quickly users could complete a reservation

Insights and Learnings

  • Shifted away from real-time public parking to strictly purchasing private spots, due to lack of incentive
  • This means switching from "push" model (users send spot updates whenever they want) to a "pull" model (users only post a spot when they want to sell it)
  • Mobile -> Web application (React.js, Vercel deployment), for increased accessibility across devices

Reflections and Post-Mortem

  • What worked: Simplified flow, decision for a web application, inclusion of Firebase for persistent data
  • What didnâ€™t: Real-time parking integration, trying to create a voluntary "push" model in the design
  • Takeaway: It's better spend more time and have a strong plan upfront, in order to prevent making major changes and redesigns
